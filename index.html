The AgroSync Mission

Bridging Agriculture and Advanced Interface Design.

AgroSync was conceptualized to solve a critical problem in modern farming: data overload. Modern farms generate terabytes of data from soil sensors, drones, and market feeds. However, legacy software traps this data in rigid tables and disjointed charts.

By applying Advanced Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) principles, AgroSync removes the "boxes" and "borders" that separate the farmer from their data. Our signature 3D Holographic Dashboard maps data directly onto a spatial representation of your actual farm, creating an intuitive, seamless workflow.

We are dedicated to building software that feels less like a spreadsheet, and more like a direct extension of your agricultural instincts.

radar How is the holographic map generated?

The core map geometry is built from topography data. The active overlay uses multi-spectral data (Red-Edge and Near-Infrared) pulled weekly from autonomous drone flights, cross-referenced with daily satellite passes. Health index is calculated using advanced NDVI and NDRE algorithms to detect chlorosis before it's visible to the human eye.

sensors Soil moisture nodes show as "Disconnected".

First, verify the primary LoRaWAN base station hub has power. If the hub is active, check the individual sensor batteries (they typically last 2 years). The system runs an auto-reconnect handshake protocol every 15 minutes. If it fails after 3 attempts, you will see the hardware alert in your task list.

auto_awesome How does AgroBrain calculate crop compatibility?

AgroBrain references your historical soil test data (pH, NPK levels, organic matter percentage) against regional climate models and historical yield data. It then ranks potential crops based on biological suitability and current market profitability margins.
